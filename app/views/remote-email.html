How it works

To take part, you will need to use a mobile smartphone or tablet . The research session will be recorded through your device.

NB: If you laptop or desktop computer, you will need to use a Chrome browser to complete the test. To do this, email fergus.roche@digital.education.gov.uk who will walk you through how to set that up.

Getting started

We're going to capture your feedback through an app that will record your phone screen, face video, and audio.

If you are using a smartphone or tablet:

  1. Open up this link PUT NEW LINK HERE. When it prompts you to install the Participate App, install it.
  2. Once the app has downloaded, tap on the above link again. This time, click on the second button that says "Participate."
  3. Follow the setup steps in the app. Make sure you allow it to access to your mic, screen, and camera please.
  4. Once you click "Start!" after completing the app setup, complete the research tasks below:

Tasks to do in the research

We have created an example of what a government service might look like. This one is about retraining to get a new job

  • Move through the different screens as if this was a service you were interested in
  • Check your skills and experience
  • Look for some training
  • Find a job
  • You will be asked some questions part of the way through the test and also at the end

    What is the research about

    You are helping the Government design a new training offer for adults. The Department for Education (DfE) is running research to help them understand how to enable more adults to get better jobs through training. We're looking for adults aged 24 and above.

    This is a great opportunity to genuinely influence government policy, and you do not need to have any prior knowledge or recent training experience to take part. We will simply be asking for your views and opinions. In addition, you may be asked to try out prototypes of the new service.
